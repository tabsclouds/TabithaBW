Statement

Tabitha Beresford-Webb tabithabw5@gmail.com

My artistic practice centres around an exploration into how we can have tangible and physical interactions with virtual images.

My artistic practice is informed by both science and art, specifically concerning notions of time and space. From science, I take inspiration from the theories within the studies of optics, computer science and quantum mechanics (e.g. holographic universe). From art, I am inspired and informed by early experimental cinema (e.g. Buster Keaton) and cartoons (e.g. Looney Tunes) as well as fine art practices (e.g. Andy Holden, Cubism, Futurism).

In both art and scienceâ€™s endeavours to explore and unpack the complexities of time and space and our visual and physical experiences of reality (virtual images) nonsense, confusion and illogical logic prevail.

Inspired by my research, I create animations in which I combine the silliness, playfulness and the nonsensical aspects of cinema, cartoons, art and science as my own attempt to make (non)sense of reality perceived through virtual images. Ultimately, my artwork explores the effects the ever-growing digital world has on the human experience of visual and physical reality.
